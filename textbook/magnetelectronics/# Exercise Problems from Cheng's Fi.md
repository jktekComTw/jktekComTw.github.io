## Study Strategy for Problems

### Difficulty Levels:
- **Odd-numbered problems:** Generally more straightforward, good for initial practice
- **Even-numbered problems:** Often more challenging or involve multiple concepts
- **Problems near end of each section:** Usually more comprehensive

### Recommended Approach:
1. **Start with odd-numbered problems** in each section to build confidence
2. **Progress to even-numbered problems** to deepen understanding
3. **Tackle comprehensive problems** at the end for integration of concepts
4. **Work additional problems** if more practice is needed in specific areas

### Problem Selection Notes:
- The specific problem numbers listed above are **recommendations** based on coverage of key concepts
- You should adjust based on your background and areas where you need more practice
- Some chapters have more problems than others - allocate time accordingly
- **Always attempt the Review Questions** at the end of each chapter before starting problems

---

## Working Through Problems

### Before Starting:
1. Review the relevant theory sections
2. Study worked examples in the chapter
3. Have formula sheets ready for reference

### While Solving:
1. Read the problem statement carefully
2. Draw diagrams and label all quantities
3. Identify known and unknown variables
4. Choose appropriate coordinate system
5. Write down relevant equations
6. Solve systematically with clear steps
7. Check units throughout
8. Verify answer makes physical sense

### After Solving:
1. Compare your method with textbook examples
2. Consider alternative solution approaches
3. Note any conceptual difficulties
4. Review related theory if needed
